What's EO ?

Earth observation (EO) is the gathering of information about the physical, chemical, and biological systems of the planet Earth. It can be performed via remote-sensing technologies (Earth observation satellites) or through direct-contact sensors in ground-based or airborne platforms (such as weather stations and weather balloons, for example). Earth Observations (EO) can help policymakers around the world make more informed decisions to address natural disasters, land management issues, the impacts of climate change, and other environmental issues.
